## After WAIC2026! 3 Real AI Trends

After visiting the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference, the biggest takeaway: AI has completely moved away from concept inflation and fully entered an era of practical implementation. This year's entire exhibition can be condensed into three keywords: scenario-specific, embodied, and application-oriented.

![Article image 1](/media/waic2026-guang-wan-jin-nian-ai-de-3-ge-zhen-shi-qu-shi-che-d/e32776111c734681985c3180bfa7c141.jpg)

### 1. Scenario-Specific: General AI Cools Down, Vertical AI Truly Lands

In previous years, everyone competed on large model parameters and benchmark scores. This year, the trend has completely shifted. General large models have become infrastructure, and the industry no longer pursues "universal AI" but rather professional AI tailored to specific industries. Mature industry models and business agents have emerged in verticals such as industry, government, healthcare, education, and finance. These are no longer abstract demonstrations but are directly embedded into workflows to solve real pain points. Simply put: AI no longer forces businesses to adapt to models; instead, models actively adapt to businesses.

### 2. Embodied: Robots Move from "Performance" to "Work"

This year, embodied intelligence is the absolute protagonist. The biggest change: humanoid robots are no longer just dancing for show; they have begun to possess real operational capabilities. Relying on world models and edge-side perception, robots can autonomously recognize environments, plan actions, and complete continuous complex tasks. The implementation forms for industrial inspection, warehouse sorting, special operations, and commercial services have become very mature. 2026 is truly the year embodied intelligence moves from prototype to mass production.

![Article image 2](/media/waic2026-guang-wan-jin-nian-ai-de-3-ge-zhen-shi-qu-shi-che-d/19e55c4b6ab34adca664dbc1b55d4053.jpg)

### 3. Application-Oriented: AI Transforms from "Tool" to "Universal Necessity"

The most intuitive feeling: the barrier to AI has significantly lowered. It's no longer something only big companies can afford; ordinary businesses and individuals can directly use mature AI applications. Office agents, edge AI, in-vehicle intelligence, and lightweight computing solutions are now widespread. AI is no longer a lofty technology but a universal productivity tool permeating offices, daily life, and industries, with implementation speed far exceeding expectations.

![Article image 3](/media/waic2026-guang-wan-jin-nian-ai-de-3-ge-zhen-shi-qu-shi-che-d/971d4cfe42c2418ea5c702903de7c918.jpg)

## Summary

The clearest signal from WAIC2026: the AI industry is debubbling, emphasizing scenarios, hardware, and implementation. The future competition is no longer about technological gimmicks but about who can truly solve industry problems and deliver real applications.
